# Frequently Asked Questions

## Can foreigners buy land in Indonesia?

Foreigners cannot own freehold land (Hak Milik) in Indonesia. However, there are legal structures that provide secure long-term access:

1. **PT PMA (Foreign-owned company):** Register an Indonesian LLC with foreign ownership. The company holds the land title (Hak Guna Bangunan — Right to Build) for 30 years, extendable to 80 years. This is the most common and recommended structure for investment.

2. **Leasehold (Hak Sewa):** Long-term lease directly from the landowner, typically 25-30 years with extension options. Simpler setup, lower cost, but less control.

3. **Hak Pakai (Right to Use):** Available to foreigners with a valid visa (KITAS/KITAP). 30-year term, renewable. Good for personal residential use.

**Our recommendation:** PT PMA for investment properties, Hak Pakai for personal use.

## How much does setting up a PT PMA cost?

Approximately $1,600-4,000 for company registration, including:
- Notary fees
- Ministry of Law & Human Rights registration
- Tax registration (NPWP)
- Business licenses (NIB via OSS)
- Minimum paid-up capital: IDR 2.5 billion (~$150-170K) per business activity (reduced from IDR 10 billion under BKPM Regulation No. 5/2025)
- Total investment plan: IDR 10 billion (~$700K), excluding land and buildings — can be in the form of capital equipment or cash

Setup timeline: approximately 2-4 weeks via OSS system.

We work with trusted legal partners who handle the entire process.

## What are the ongoing costs?

- Annual company tax filing: ~$500-800/year
- Land tax (PBB): very low, typically $50-200/year depending on plot size
- Company maintenance (accountant, annual report): ~$1,000/year

## Is Sumbawa safe?

Yes. Sumbawa is a peaceful, predominantly Muslim island with low crime rates. The local Sumbawan people are welcoming to foreigners. There's a growing expat community in West Sumbawa, especially around the surf coast (Kertasari, Jelenga, Maluk, Sekongkang).

## How do I visit the plots?

1. Fly to Bali (DPS) or Lombok (LOP) — international flights worldwide
2. Lombok → Poto Tano ferry (90 min fast ferry / 2h regular) — this is the main route to West Sumbawa
3. We arrange pickup at Poto Tano ferry terminal and guided tour of selected plots
4. All West Sumbawa plots are within ~1 hour of Poto Tano along the surf coast
5. Typical visit: 2-3 days to see all areas

Future: Kiantar Airport (under construction at Poto Tano) will offer direct domestic flights from Bali and Jakarta.

## What's the buying process timeline?

1. **Plot selection & negotiation** — 1-2 weeks
2. **Due diligence** (certificate check, zoning) — 1-2 weeks
3. **Legal structure setup** (PT PMA if needed) — 4-6 weeks
4. **Land transaction & notarization** — 1-2 weeks
5. **Certificate transfer** — 2-4 weeks

**Total: approximately 2-3 months** from decision to completed purchase.

## Can I build on the land?

Yes, but you need:
- IMB (Building Permit) — now called PBG under OSS system
- Approved building plans from a licensed architect
- Environmental assessment for plots >5,000 m² or near coastline

We can recommend architects experienced with Sumbawa builds.

## Can I rent out my property?

Yes. Especially along West Sumbawa's surf coast (Kertasari, Jelenga, Maluk, Sekongkang), there's strong demand for:
- Surf camp accommodation (Scar Reef, Yoyo's, Super Suck attract international surfers)
- Villa rentals (Airbnb model)
- Long-term expat rentals
- Dive resort accommodation (manta ray diving near Kertasari)

Beachfront and surf zone plots have the highest rental potential.

## What if I want to sell later?

The market is growing. Exit strategies include:
- Sell the PT PMA company (including land) to another buyer
- Transfer the lease to a new lessee
- Sell to Indonesian buyers (broader market)
- Developer buyout (as the area develops)
