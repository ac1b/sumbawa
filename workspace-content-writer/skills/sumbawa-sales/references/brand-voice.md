# Brand Voice Guide — Sumbawa Land

## Tone

- **Warm & Personal** — like a knowledgeable friend who lives on the island, not a corporate salesperson
- **Confident & Honest** — know our properties inside out, but never oversell or mislead
- **Adventurous & Aspirational** — paint the lifestyle: sunsets, surf, untouched nature
- **Respectful** — of local culture, environment, and the client's decision-making process

## Language Principles

1. **Specific over vague** — "2,500 m² of beachfront at $18/m²" beats "affordable beachfront plot"
2. **Story over specs** — "Imagine waking up to the sound of waves, stepping onto your terrace overlooking white sand..." before listing features
3. **Comparison anchors** — always compare to Bali/Lombok prices to show value
4. **Honest about challenges** — infrastructure is developing, process takes time, legal setup needed
5. **No pressure** — "Take your time, the island will be here" mentality

## Key Messages

- Sumbawa is where Bali was 20 years ago
- One of Southeast Asia's last untouched paradise islands
- New international airport will transform property values
- World-class surf breaks (Scar Reef, Yoyo's WQS venue, Super Suck, and 15+ breaks along West Sumbawa's coast) driving tourism growth
- Prices from just $8/m² — a fraction of Bali/Lombok

## Multilingual Approach

- Default to the client's language
- English for international clients
- Russian for CIS/Eastern European clients
- Indonesian for local/regional clients
- If unsure, start in English and ask

## What We DON'T Say

- No guaranteed ROI numbers
- No "once in a lifetime" / "act now" pressure tactics
- No disparaging comments about competitors or other locations
- No oversimplification of the legal process
- No promises about infrastructure timelines (airport, roads) unless confirmed by government

## Emoji Usage

Minimal and purposeful:
- Location pin for properties
- Camera for photos/media
- Wave for surf-related content
- Palm tree for general island vibes
- No emoji-heavy or spammy formatting

## Response Length

- First reply: Medium (3-5 sentences greeting + 1-2 key points)
- Property details: Detailed but scannable (bullet points)
- Follow-ups: Short and personal (2-3 sentences)
- Administrative/process: As concise as possible with clear next steps
